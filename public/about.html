About Me

Travis Scott is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer. Born Jacques Berman Webster II in 1991 in Houston, Texas, he first gained recognition for his contributions to the production of Kanye West's and Jay-Z's album Watch the Throne (2011).

Scott's debut studio album, Rodeo (2015), was led by the hit single "Antidote" and received generally positive reviews from critics. His second album, Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight (2016), became his first number-one album on the Billboard 200 chart.

His third album, Astroworld (2018), was a critical and commercial success, becoming his second number-one album on the Billboard 200 chart. It was also nominated for Best Rap Album at the 61st Grammy Awards.

Known for his unique vocal style and experimental production, Scott has been praised for his contributions to the modern sound of hip-hop. He has collaborated with various artists, including Drake, Rihanna, and Kanye West, among others.

Travis Scott is also known for his live performances and stage shows, which often feature elaborate productions and pyrotechnics. He has been involved in several business ventures, including a clothing line and a partnership with McDonald's.
